Chamisa MacKenzie, LMSW

Psychotherapist

About Me

I have worked in service roles for over ten years, and have found my passion as a psychotherapist. My commitment to service, social justice, and equality was instilled growing up in the Quaker community and reinforced in my academic career at the small liberal arts Quaker College, Earlham. Following graduation I worked for several years as an Early Head Start Home Visitor in Portland, Oregon, and then joined the United States Peace Corps and served for 3 years in Costa Rica. My early professional and volunteering experiences deepened my commitment to finding ways to support and nurture individual and community capacity for growth and change. These experiences led me back to graduate school at the University of Michigan where I earned a Masters in Social Work (MSW) and a Masters in Public Health (MPH). I completed a 2-year post-graduate clinical fellowship at the University of Michigan Department of Psychiatry and am now a member of their clinical staff. I see clients privately in downtown Ann Arbor one day a week.

My Approach

As a therapist I feel privileged to be a part of your journey towards healing and wellness. I work from a strengths-based perspective, helping each person to find and connect with their own innate wisdom, while providing opportunities to learn and practice more effective coping and communication skills. My goal is to help you address both specific problems as well as understand and navigate the underlying causes of your suffering.

My own practice of mindfulness allows me to be fully present and attuned to your emotional needs while creating an atmosphere of support, safety and calm.

I have training in a wide range of modalities including, Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT), Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Motivational Interviewing, Mindfulness, and Trauma-Informed therapies.

My areas of clinical interest and specialty include trauma, anxiety, depression, obsessive compulsive disorder, family of origin issues, relationship difficulties, and navigating challenging emotions.

I work with and welcome adult individuals of all races, religions, genders, sexual orientation and cultures.

Contact Me

You may call or email for more information about my practice or to set up an appointment.​

Pricing & Billing

My practice is fee for service and my rate is $130/hour. I do not accept insurance, however, depending on your insurance provider, you may be able to submit for reimbursement for "Out of Network" mental health coverage. Please contact your insurance provider for specifics.
